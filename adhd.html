Understanding Adult ADHD

Attention Deficit Hyperactivity Disorder (ADHD) is a neurodevelopmental condition that affects executive functioning, attention, and impulse control. While often diagnosed in childhood, many adults continue to experience symptoms that impact work, relationships, and daily functioning.

Key Symptoms in Adults

Inattention: Difficulty maintaining focus, frequent task switching, forgetfulness, and poor time management.

Hyperactivity: Restlessness, difficulty sitting still, excessive talking, and feeling constantly "on the go."

Impulsivity: Acting without thinking, interrupting others, making hasty decisions, and difficulty waiting.

Our Comprehensive ADHD Evaluation Process

Dr. Todd Allen provides thorough ADHD evaluations using evidence-based assessment methods and clinical expertise developed over years of practice.

Evaluation Components

Clinical Interview: Detailed discussion of symptoms, developmental history, and current challenges.

Standardized Assessments: Validated rating scales and questionnaires to measure ADHD symptoms.

Differential Diagnosis: Careful consideration of other conditions that may mimic ADHD symptoms.

Functional Assessment: Evaluation of how symptoms impact work, relationships, and daily activities.

Personalized Treatment Approaches

Treatment plans are tailored to each individual's specific needs, symptoms, and lifestyle considerations.

Medication Management

Stimulant Medications: First-line treatment options including methylphenidate and amphetamine-based medications.

Non-Stimulant Options: Alternative medications for those who don't respond to or tolerate stimulants.

Careful Monitoring: Regular follow-up to assess effectiveness and adjust dosages as needed.
