Understanding Anxiety Disorders

Anxiety disorders are characterized by excessive worry, fear, and nervousness that interfere with daily functioning. While some anxiety is normal, persistent and overwhelming anxiety can significantly impact quality of life and requires professional treatment.

Common Anxiety Disorders

Generalized Anxiety Disorder (GAD): Persistent worry about various aspects of life, often accompanied by physical symptoms like restlessness and fatigue.

Panic Disorder: Recurrent panic attacks with intense fear and physical symptoms like heart palpitations and shortness of breath.

Social Anxiety Disorder: Intense fear of social situations and being judged by others, leading to avoidance of social interactions.

Medication Management for Anxiety

Medication can be an effective component of anxiety treatment, helping to reduce symptoms and improve overall functioning.

Medication Options

Selective Serotonin Reuptake Inhibitors (SSRIs): First-line treatment for most anxiety disorders, with good safety and effectiveness profiles.

Serotonin-Norepinephrine Reuptake Inhibitors (SNRIs): Alternative option for individuals who don't respond to SSRIs.

Benzodiazepines: Short-term use for acute anxiety symptoms, carefully monitored to avoid dependence.

Buspirone: Non-addictive medication specifically for generalized anxiety disorder.
