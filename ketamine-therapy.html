Note: This content is for educational purposes only and does not constitute medical advice. Treatment decisions should be made in consultation with Dr. Allen based on your individual circumstances, medical history, and treatment goals.

What Is Ketamine Therapy?

Ketamine therapy is an FDA-approved treatment for treatment-resistant depression that works differently from traditional antidepressants. While conventional medications can take weeks or months to show effects, ketamine therapy often provides rapid relief from depressive symptoms‚Äîsometimes within hours or days.

Originally used as an anesthetic, ketamine has been found to have powerful antidepressant effects when administered at lower doses in a controlled medical setting. It works by targeting the brain's glutamate system, which is different from the serotonin and norepinephrine pathways targeted by traditional antidepressants.

For many patients who haven't found relief with traditional antidepressants, ketamine therapy has provided hope and significant symptom improvement. During your consultation, Dr. Allen will help you understand whether ketamine therapy might be an appropriate option for your unique situation.

Who Might Consider Ketamine Therapy?

You may consider ketamine therapy if you:

  • Have tried multiple antidepressant medications without significant improvement
  • Continue to experience depressive symptoms despite medication treatment
  • Are experiencing treatment-resistant depression
  • Are interested in exploring alternative treatment approaches

Important: Ketamine therapy is not appropriate for everyone, and it's just one of many treatment options available. During your consultation with Dr. Allen, you'll discuss your specific situation, medical history, and treatment goals to determine which approach‚Äîor combination of approaches‚Äîmight be most suitable for you.

How Ketamine Therapy Works for Depression

Ketamine is a rapid-acting antidepressant that works by blocking NMDA receptors in the brain, which triggers increased production of brain-derived neurotrophic factor (BDNF)‚Äîa protein essential for brain health. This protein helps repair and strengthen neural connections that may have been damaged by chronic depression.

Unlike traditional antidepressants that can take 4-8 weeks to show effects, ketamine therapy can provide relief within hours to days. This rapid response can be life-changing for those who have been suffering from severe, treatment-resistant depression.

How It's Different

Traditional antidepressants work by increasing levels of serotonin or norepinephrine in the brain, while ketamine works on the glutamate system‚Äîa completely different mechanism of action. This is why it can be effective when other treatments have failed.

Safety & Side Effects

Ketamine therapy is generally safe when administered by a qualified medical professional in a controlled setting. However, like all medical treatments, it can have side effects.

Common Side Effects During Treatment:

  • Mild dissociation or feeling "out of body"
  • Dizziness or lightheadedness
  • Nausea (usually mild)
  • Blurred vision
  • Increased blood pressure or heart rate

These effects typically subside within 1-2 hours after treatment. You'll be monitored throughout the session to ensure your safety and comfort.

Contraindications:

  • Those with uncontrolled high blood pressure
  • Individuals with certain heart conditions
  • People with a history of psychosis or certain psychiatric conditions
  • Those with active substance abuse issues
  • Pregnant or breastfeeding women

Dr. Allen will conduct a thorough evaluation to ensure ketamine therapy is safe and appropriate for you.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is ketamine therapy covered by insurance in Utah?

Coverage varies by insurance plan and provider. Some Utah plans cover ketamine therapy for treatment-resistant depression, while others may require prior authorization. We recommend contacting your insurance provider and our office at (385) 504-1334 to discuss your specific coverage.

How many ketamine treatments are needed?

Most patients receive an initial series of 4-6 treatments over 2-3 weeks. After the initial series, maintenance treatments may be recommended based on your response. The exact number of treatments varies by individual.

What are the side effects of ketamine therapy?

Common side effects during treatment include mild dissociation, dizziness, nausea, blurred vision, and temporary increases in blood pressure or heart rate. These typically subside within 1-2 hours. You'll be closely monitored throughout treatment for your safety.

Who is a good candidate for ketamine therapy?

Good candidates are typically those who have tried at least two different antidepressant medications without significant improvement (treatment-resistant depression). However, each person's situation is unique. A comprehensive evaluation with Dr. Allen will determine if ketamine therapy is appropriate for you.

How long does ketamine therapy take?

Each treatment session typically lasts 40-60 minutes, plus time for monitoring and recovery. The initial treatment series usually spans 2-3 weeks with treatments 2-3 times per week.

How quickly does ketamine therapy work?

Unlike traditional antidepressants that can take weeks, ketamine therapy can provide relief within hours to days. Many patients notice improvement after the first or second treatment.

Do I need to stop my current medications?

Not necessarily. Dr. Allen will review your current medications and determine the best approach. Many patients continue their existing medications while receiving ketamine therapy.

What's the difference between ketamine and Spravato (esketamine)?

Spravato (esketamine) is a nasal spray form of ketamine that's FDA-approved for treatment-resistant depression. It's a specific chemical form (S-ketamine) of the ketamine molecule. IV ketamine infusion therapy (what we offer) uses racemic ketamine, which contains both forms. Both can be effective, but they're administered differently. During your consultation, Dr. Allen can discuss which approach might be more suitable for your situation.
